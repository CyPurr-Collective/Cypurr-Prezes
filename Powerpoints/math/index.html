Breaking Passwords

As of 2011, it was possible to test 3 billion passwords per second!

 

That means that a random password 10 characters long can be broken in 1 day!

 

Georgia Tech Research Institute recommends 12 characters at least.

This website is a giant database of leaked and or exposed passwords, stored in their cryptographic form. They do not share the actual passwords, but allow you to safely check your password against a known list.

They have 154 million records, spanning many websites and services.

Tools for Managing Passwords

Password Manager

A password manager stores your strong password

Browser

Modern browsers like Chrome and Firefox store passwords for you and save them behind a single password.

 

It is especially important that this password is very strong!!!

2-FA

2-FA (Two-Factor Authentication) is a way of protecting your accounts and data using two factors.

Most of the time, your 10 digit phone number or one-time code sent to your email address are used as the 2nd factor.

Much better than passwords alone, but still subject to some risk.

U2f

U2F (Universal Two-Factor) replaces the one time code with a cryptographically secure system that you carry with you.

 

It's a literal key to your data.
