Polyalphabetic Cipher

Uses a 26 different alphabets and a passphrase that tells the decoder which lines to use when.

Described by Charles Babbage as in use during the Crimean War of the 1850s. He broke a cipher known as the Vigenere cipher. s

Used throughout the middle ages, with the oldest known record  in 1553. Was known as 'indecipherable.'

World War II: Short Weather Cipher

This WW2 era encryption scheme was used by German U-boat captains to navigate their ships and report intelligence.

Enigma Machine

Uses a series of rotors to encipher text, turning one letter into another using a one-time code.

Eventually broken by MI6, led by Alan Turing

Weaknesses

 

The letters used in any given language are not randomly distributed. Given sufficient plaintext, we could use frequency analysis to break a code.

 

Additionally, codes needed to be pre-shared, allowing for another, easier attack vector.

Discrete Logarithm Problem


Let G be any group. Let b be any element in G. For k = 0, the kth power is  the identity b^0 =1. Let a also be an element of G. An integer, k, that solves the equation b^k = a is termed a discrete logarithm. In general this is written as.

k = \text{log}_b a
k=logba k = \text{log}_b ak=logb​a

Try it

k = \text{log}_2 4
k=log24 k = \text{log}_2 4k=log2​4

Why might this be important?

By using a large prime, this formula becomes very hard to solve, but relatively easy to verify.

 

The 2015 Logjam attack used this vulnerability to compromise a variety of Internet services that allowed the use of groups whose order was a 512-bit prime number, so called export grade.

Information Theory (Shannon)

Invented in 1948 by Claude Shannon as he investigated the fundamental limits on signal processing.

 

Was a precursor to all modern, digital systems.

 

Entropy is a measure of how much predictability there is. Low entropy is bad for cryptographic systems.

 

The entropy of a Bernoulli trial as a function of success probability, often called the binary entropy function, Hb(p). The entropy is maximized at 1 bit per trial when the two possible outcomes are equally probable, as in an unbiased coin toss.

Uses a well-known public key from both recipients to encrypt communications to each other.

 

It uses the secrete private key to decrypt the messages.
